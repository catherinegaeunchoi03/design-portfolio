01 — The brief

What problem were we solving?

New Era's UK and EU storefronts were running on an ageing Shopify theme with inconsistent campaign rollouts and a homepage that prioritised product categories over brand storytelling. With seasonal campaigns driving the majority of traffic, the existing setup made it difficult to launch quickly or maintain visual consistency across markets.

My brief was to improve the homepage experience, build a repeatable system for campaign landing pages, and make front-end amendments that brought the live site closer to brand standards — all within the constraints of the existing Shopify CMS without custom development resource.

02 — Process

How I approached it

Working within Shopify's theme editor meant every design decision had to account for what was technically possible without breaking the existing template. I mapped the current homepage journey first — identifying where users dropped off, which blocks were underperforming, and where brand inconsistencies were most visible.

From there I designed iteratively — proposing layouts in Figma, getting sign-off, then implementing directly in the theme editor with HTML/CSS amendments where the native controls weren't sufficient.

03 — Homepage redesign

Redesigning the primary entry point

The original homepage led with a static product category grid — visually flat and disconnected from the brand's campaign-led identity. I redesigned the hero block to lead with campaign creative, using a full-width editorial layout that put the seasonal story front and centre.

Secondary sections were restructured to guide users from editorial context into product discovery, reflecting how the majority of visitors were actually arriving — via campaign traffic, already primed for a specific drop or collection.

04 — Campaign pages

Building a repeatable campaign template

Each campaign previously required rebuilding a landing page from scratch — slow, inconsistent, and dependent on whoever happened to be available. I designed and built a modular campaign landing page template as a custom Shopify theme block, allowing the team to launch new campaigns by swapping content rather than rebuilding structure.

The template was flexible enough to handle different campaign types — product launches, seasonal drops, collaborations — while maintaining consistent visual hierarchy and brand standards across UK and EU.

05 — Front-end work

Closing the gap between design and live

Shopify's native theme editor has real limits — spacing, typography, and layout controls that stop short of what a precise design system requires. Where the CMS fell short, I made direct HTML and CSS amendments to bring the live site into alignment with brand standards.

This included custom theme block development using Shopify's Liquid templating language, front-end fixes for cross-browser rendering issues, and responsive layout adjustments to ensure consistency across mobile and desktop — particularly important given a majority of traffic arrived on mobile.

07 — Reflection

What I'd do differently

Working within Shopify's constraints taught me a lot about designing for systems rather than ideal conditions — every decision had to account for what the CMS could actually render, which forced a kind of pragmatic rigour I wouldn't have developed designing in isolation.

If I owned this project longer, I'd have pushed for more structured user testing earlier in the process. Most decisions were informed by analytics and business feedback rather than direct user insight — which worked, but left questions about whether the hierarchy we chose truly matched how users were thinking, not just how they were clicking.

I'd also have advocated for a more formal design system documentation from the start — the template approach solved the immediate problem, but without documented standards, variations crept in over time as other team members made edits.

"Designing within a CMS taught me that constraints aren't the enemy of good design — they're a forcing function. The best solutions I found weren't despite Shopify's limits, they were shaped by them."
